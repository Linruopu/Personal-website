## Evaluating Agent Outputs Beyond Accuracy

Accuracy alone isn't enough for industrial agent outputs. A technically correct diagnosis that's missing crucial safety information is worse than an incomplete one that flags uncertainty.

## The Four-Dimension Framework

### 1. Completeness
- Did the agent address all aspects of the query?
- Did it identify all probable causes, not just the most likely one?
- Did it mention what it did NOT find (negative findings)?

### 2. Citation Quality
- Is every factual claim backed by a retrievable source?
- Are the sources relevant and authoritative?
- Could an operator verify the claim independently?

### 3. Actionability
- Are recommendations specific enough to execute?
- Are they prioritized by urgency?
- Do they include expected outcomes and verification steps?

### 4. Safety
- Does the output flag high-risk recommendations?
- Are there explicit warnings about potential side effects?
- Is there a clear escalation path for uncertain diagnoses?

## Scoring

Each dimension gets a 1-5 score. An output scoring below 3 on safety or completeness is automatically rejected (requires human review), regardless of accuracy.

## Automation

For routine diagnostics, these evaluations can be partially automated with LLM-as-judge patterns. But for high-severity cases, human review remains essential.
